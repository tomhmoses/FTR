Prince Llewellyn of Gwynedd and Conwy had a faithful dog named Gelert whom he loved greatly. He so trusted Gelert that whilst hunting one day he left Gelert to guard his only son and heir. However, when the Prince returned he saw that the room was broken! the baby gone! and Gelert was covered in blood.
Bewildered, betrayed and deeply grieved, Llewellyn turned his sword on his once trusted and beloved animal.
But as faithful Gelert drew his last breath, a cry was heard. Hunting the room, the Prince found the body of a great wolf, and hiding in the fur, covered in blood but unharmed was his child, whom Gelert had saved from the wild beast.
Realising that he had murdered his most faithful servant, Llewellyn is said to never have smiled again. He buried Gelert by the river Glaslyn, and left a great stone so that all that passed that place may know of the bravery of Gelert.
