hi guys where in south france are you planning to go to? i am there now (in ceuse) and have talked to many euro climbers and the only crag there im aware of that is climbable in summer is ceuse. sorry to be a wet blanket. http://www.ukclimbing.com/articles/page.php?id=118

i have been to gorges du tarn for and past gorges du verdon and arnot etc and they are unbearably hot. we stayed at tarn for 2 days and decided to bail because it was a total waste of time. Only ceuse is high altitude and there are always climbing faces in the shade. in the south its impossible to shag in the sun even at high altitude. hot means 35 deg celcius and im from the tropics.

if you go to ceuse there is only one camping site climbers use. Les Guerins. They rent caravans (quite crummy) for 6 euro a night per person if available or else its 5 euro to camp. On the plus side they have fridges. The only downside is the walk up to the crag, 1hr and very sweaty. Also the grades are stiff. The routes are top notch and there are many fun climbs to be had in the 6a and beyond range. Be prepared for crimps n pockets and bring your best rock shoes.

let me know if you want more info.
