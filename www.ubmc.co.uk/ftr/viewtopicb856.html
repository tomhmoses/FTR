Just to point out, I pulled up to creation at the same time as an ambulance with flashing lights. There to carry out a customer who had a bone sticking 2inches out of his ankle..... Messy.


On arrival, the wall is painted in very bold colours and is definitely an attractive looking bouldering area. Although it doesn't have the appeal which centres like the TCA have on first arrival, it is not solely a bouldering venue so I wouldn't expect this.

Firstly the routes are set in 7 different circuits. Green, being the easiest working up to Black/grey being the hardest. Circuits have been very popular in other centres. They work particularly well at the Works and TCA.

Putting the excitement of having a brand new wall on my doorstep behind me, I was rather dissappointed with the diversity of the routes. Every circuit uses similar holds. For example the orange circuit, are all grippy/pinchy holds, and the blacks being mainly crimpers. One of the things I like most about other centres including Redpoint, is the variety of different holds. The most recent example of the broad set of holds they have over at the rival centre is the big orange and black blob route. These holds are huge but the climber has to find the best way to hold them in the circumstances. Makes for a really interesting battle.

I would assume that the holds will start to vary when some of the holds from the existing bouldering section and top-roped walls get intergrated into the new area.

One other dissappointment is the lack of screw-on's and screws in large holds(to stop them spinning.). Hopefully the 'screw-ban' will only be for a few months. I also hope to see some of the old volumes and some new ones on the walls in the coming months.

Most of the new routes seem to be like ladders(an obvious line of holds). You can see the way but your not strong enough to hold on to a certain hold. Other centres try to avoid this to try and make climbs as similar to routes outdoors where it depends on a certain technique/way of doing it, which is not obvious. This said, some of the routes on the new slab area are very good.

Lastly, the mats are currently brand new and therefore VERY solid. Infact I was more worried going for the last move @ creation then I usually am climbing high outside. Make sure you land well when climbing there and if your going for a hard last move, get a spotter!!

On saying all of this, there are still quite a good set of routes to try, loads of new shapes, and especially that huge new roof installed, makes for continually interesting campusing.

All in all, not bad, but I cannot wait for Redpoints new bouldering section to be unleashed. I think it's ridiculous that we get two great centres within 10minutes of each other. And at bargain price. Some of the London wall's cost Â£13 a session!!!
