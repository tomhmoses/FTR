I have also budgeted for a possible 1 day course at Milton Keynes to get to grips with this slippery mode of transport. I'm well aware that I won't be any good, and I fully expect to spend most of my holiday time on nursery slopes/falling over and, with a birthday party involved: drunk!

Now to the big debate: Some say ski, some say board. An an equal amount of my friends are standing each side of fence!

Looking to the future, and if I'm making this kind of financial investment, I want to get alright at this snowy sport thats for sure! - Ski touring seemed to hold out over boarding as such an off-piste adventure was 'not possible'. However! I have now come across split-boarding! Where you 'ski-tour' on a split board, then fit it together to board down the mountain!

So! Which one should I learn to do at Milton Keynes? - In an ideal world, I'd want to learn both, but I cant. :/

I don't think splitboards are that new really, I think they've been round for a decade or so, the problem is they're a massive hassle, whereas on touring ski's you just put your skins on, release your heel and go, on split boards you have to split the board, swivel your binding, release your heel, then put skins on.

Also because boards are short the two "skis" you get from a split board are really short, which is a major problem in deep snow, i.e. it makes going uphill really hard/impossible, and deep snow is what you aim for!
