Soo Guan:

It doesn't matter how much previous experience of climbing you have - the point of our club is to cater for anyone who would like to be part of a climbing club - complete beginners will be taught what they need to know and those already proficient can simply join in, so don't worry about how much experience you have!

On the subject of clothes: climbing actually involves a lot of sitting and standing around, which can soon make you very cold outside! The best thing is to have lots of layers so that you can add or remove clothes depending on how warm it is - so if you only have one jumper, it would be good to wear a few of your other warmest tops underneath. If you have a warm coat that it is ok to get a bit dirty, take that aswell. Waterproofs are always useful as they cut out the wind, which helps to keep you warm, so I would reccomend bringing a waterproof coat if you have one. We don't plan to be outside in torrential rain, so waterproof trousers aren't essential.

Other than that, bring what I've put on the kitlist. Foodwise, bring water to keep you hydrated and enough food to keep you going all day (I usually bring a sandwich, chocolate bars, a banana and another piece of fruit) and something that you can share with your driver as a thankyou for driving.
